Chhattisgarh

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 November 2000

Capital: Raipur

Geography & Climate

Carved out of Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh is heavily forested and is known as the "Rice Bowl of India" due to its rich agricultural plains. The state features the dense forests of Bastar in the south and is crisscrossed by the Mahanadi River.

History & Culture

The region has a deeply rooted tribal culture, home to indigenous groups like the Gonds and Baigas. It is known for its unique tribal art, including Dokra (bell metal) casting, and vibrant traditional festivals that celebrate nature and local deities.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Chhattisgarh has a population of approximately 25.5 million. It is one of India's most mineral-rich states, providing a massive share of the country's coal, iron ore, and steel production.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Chitrakote Waterfalls: Often called the "Niagara Falls of India," located on the Indravati River.
  • Bastar: Famous for its dense forests, tribal culture, and the grand Bastar Dussehra.
  • Sirpur: An ancient historical site featuring Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments.
  • Raipur: The bustling capital city blending modern industry with ancient temples.

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official language)
  • Chhattisgarhi (widely spoken regional language)
  • Gondi and Halbi (spoken in tribal regions)
