Tripura

Basic Information

Formed on: 21 January 1972

Capital: Agartala

Geography & Climate

Tripura is a small state in Northeast India, geographically unique because it is bordered by Bangladesh on three sides. The state features five parallel hill ranges running north to south, interspersed with valleys. The climate is tropical savanna, marked by heavy monsoon rains.

History & Culture

Before merging with India in 1949, Tripura was an ancient princely state ruled by the Manikya dynasty for centuries. The culture is a unique blend of indigenous tribal traditions (with 19 distinct tribes) and Bengali culture, celebrated through diverse music, dance, and colorful festivals like Kharchi Puja.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Tripura has a population of approximately 3.7 million. The state is highly agrarian, with rubber and tea being major cash crops. It is also famous for its exquisite bamboo and cane handicraft industries.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Agartala: The capital city, known for its grand royal palaces and museums.
  • Neermahal: A stunning royal water palace built right in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake.
  • Ujjayanta Palace: The former royal palace, which now serves as the state's largest museum.
  • Unakoti: An ancient Hindu pilgrimage site featuring massive, spectacular rock-cut carvings in the forest.

Languages Spoken

  • Bengali (official language and mother tongue for the majority)
  • Kokborok (official language of the indigenous Tripuri people)
  • English
