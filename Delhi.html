Delhi (National Capital Territory)

Basic Information

Established as NCT: 1 February 1992

Capital: New Delhi

Geography & Climate

Located in northern India, Delhi is landlocked and bordered by Haryana and Uttar Pradesh. The city sits along the banks of the Yamuna River. It experiences an extreme climate, with scorching summers and chilly winters.

History & Culture

Delhi is one of the oldest continually inhabited cities in the world. It has served as the capital for numerous empires, most notably the Delhi Sultanate and the Mughals, before being rebuilt as New Delhi by the British. This incredible layered history makes it a global center for architecture, food, and culture.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Delhi has a population of approximately 16.8 million, making it one of the largest metropolitan areas in the world. It is the political hub of India and possesses a massive, diverse economy driven by IT, telecommunications, and services.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Red Fort & Jama Masjid: Iconic architectural marvels built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan.
  • India Gate: A majestic war memorial located in the heart of New Delhi.
  • Qutub Minar: A towering 12th-century brick minaret and UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Lotus Temple: A Bahá'í House of Worship known for its unique flower-like shape.

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official and most widely spoken)
  • English (used in administration and business)
  • Punjabi
  • Urdu
