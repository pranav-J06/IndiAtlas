Manipur

Basic Information

Formed on: 21 January 1972

Capital: Imphal

Geography & Climate

Located in Northeast India, Manipur is often described as the "Jeweled Land." It features an oval-shaped lush valley surrounded by majestic blue hills. The climate ranges from tropical to sub-alpine, making it incredibly rich in biodiversity and exotic flora and fauna.

History & Culture

Manipur was a princely state under British rule before officially merging with the Indian Union in 1949. It boasts a deeply artistic culture and is globally renowned for the classical Manipuri dance (Jagoi) and indigenous martial arts like Thang-Ta. The state is also recognized as the birthplace of modern polo (Sagol Kangjei).

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Manipur has a population of approximately 2.9 million. The economy relies heavily on agriculture, forestry, cottage industries, and traditional handloom weaving.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Loktak Lake: The largest freshwater lake in Northeast India, famous for its circular floating swamps called phumdis.
  • Imphal: The capital city, offering a mix of history, culture, and vibrant local markets.
  • Kangla Fort: The historical seat of the ancient Manipuri kings.
  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: The world's only floating national park, home to the endangered Sangai deer.

Languages Spoken

  • Meitei (Manipuri) – the official language and lingua franca
  • English (widely used in education)
  • Hindi
  • Various indigenous tribal languages in the hill districts
