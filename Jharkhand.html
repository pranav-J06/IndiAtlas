Jharkhand

Basic Information

Formed on: 15 November 2000

Capital: Ranchi

Geography & Climate

Carved out of the southern part of Bihar, Jharkhand translates to "Land of Forests." The state is located on the Chota Nagpur Plateau and features a rugged terrain characterized by dense woodlands, cascading waterfalls, and rich river valleys.

History & Culture

Jharkhand is renowned for its significant indigenous tribal populations, including the Santhal, Munda, and Ho tribes. The region was the epicenter of several historic tribal uprisings against British colonial rule, most notably led by the freedom fighter Birsa Munda.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Jharkhand has a population of approximately 32.9 million. It holds the largest share of India's mineral wealth, making heavy industries, coal mining, and steel production (notably in Jamshedpur and Bokaro) the backbone of its economy.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Ranchi: Known as the "City of Waterfalls," featuring Dassam and Hundru Falls.
  • Betla National Park: One of India's first national parks to become a tiger reserve.
  • Deoghar: A major Hindu pilgrimage center home to the Baidyanath Jyotirlinga temple.
  • Netarhat: A beautiful hill station known as the "Queen of Chotanagpur."

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official language)
  • Santhali, Mundari, and Ho (major tribal languages)
  • Bengali and Odia (in border districts)
