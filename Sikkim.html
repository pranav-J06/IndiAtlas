Sikkim

Basic Information

Formed on: 16 May 1975

Capital: Gangtok

Geography & Climate

Nestled deep in the Himalayas, Sikkim is India's second-smallest state. It shares international borders with Bhutan, Tibet, and Nepal. The state is characterized by dramatic mountainous terrain and is home to Kangchenjunga, the highest mountain peak in India and the third highest on Earth.

History & Culture

Sikkim was an independent kingdom ruled by the Namgyal dynasty until it officially merged with India in 1975. The culture is a peaceful, beautiful blend of Hindu and Tibetan Buddhist traditions, dotted with ancient monasteries and prayer flags.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Sikkim is the least populous state in India with approximately 0.61 million people. It is famous for being the world's first 100% organic state. The economy is driven by eco-tourism, agriculture (notably large cardamom), and hydroelectric power.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Gangtok: The scenic capital city known for its cleanliness, monasteries, and cable car rides.
  • Tsomgo (Changu) Lake: A stunning, high-altitude glacial lake considered sacred by the locals.
  • Yumthang Valley: Known as the "Valley of Flowers," offering spectacular views of snow-capped peaks.
  • Nathula Pass: A historic mountain pass on the ancient Silk Road connecting India and Tibet.

Languages Spoken

  • Nepali (official language and lingua franca)
  • Sikkimese (Bhutia)
  • Lepcha
  • English (used in administration and education)
