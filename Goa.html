Goa

Basic Information

Formed on: 30 May 1987

Capital: Panaji

Geography & Climate

Goa is India's smallest state by land area, situated on the western coast in the Konkan region. It is bound by the Arabian Sea to the west and the lush Western Ghats to the east, giving it a beautiful tropical monsoon climate.

History & Culture

Goa was a Portuguese province for over 450 years until it was annexed by India in 1961. This long period of colonial rule left a unique, indelible mark on Goan culture, blending Indian and Portuguese influences in its world-famous cuisine, music, and distinct architecture.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Goa has a population of approximately 1.46 million. It boasts the highest GDP per capita among all Indian states, with its economy heavily reliant on tourism and iron ore mining.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Baga & Calangute Beaches: Famous for water sports, shacks, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Old Goa Churches: UNESCO World Heritage sites, including the Basilica of Bom Jesus.
  • Dudhsagar Waterfalls: A spectacular four-tiered waterfall on the Mandovi River.
  • Fort Aguada: A 17th-century Portuguese fort and lighthouse overlooking the sea.

Languages Spoken

  • Konkani (official language)
  • Marathi
  • Hindi
  • English (widely understood due to tourism)
