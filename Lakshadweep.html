Lakshadweep

Basic Information

Established as UT: 1 November 1956

Capital: Kavaratti

Geography & Climate

Lakshadweep is India's smallest Union Territory. It is a breathtaking archipelago consisting of 36 islands located in the Laccadive Sea, off the southwestern coast of India. The region is famous for its stunning coral atolls, pristine white-sand beaches, and crystal-clear blue waters.

Culture & Economy

The culture of Lakshadweep is closely linked to that of the neighboring state of Kerala. It is known for its distinct Islamic heritage and peaceful island lifestyle. The local economy is heavily dependent on agriculture (primarily coconut cultivation), fishing, coir making, and a growing, strictly regulated eco-tourism industry.

Population

As per Census 2011, Lakshadweep is the least populous region in India with a population of approximately 0.064 million (64,000).

Popular Tourist Places

  • Agatti Island: Known for having the only airstrip in the territory and beautiful lagoons.
  • Kavaratti Island: The administrative capital, featuring stunning white mosques and water sports.
  • Bangaram Island: A premium tourist destination famous for its coral reefs.
  • Minicoy Island: The southernmost island, heavily influenced by Maldivian culture.

Languages Spoken

  • Malayalam (official and most widely spoken)
  • Jeseri (a dialect of Malayalam)
  • Mahl (spoken primarily in Minicoy)
  • English
