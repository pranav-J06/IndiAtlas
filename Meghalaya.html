Meghalaya

Basic Information

Formed on: 21 January 1972

Capital: Shillong

Geography & Climate

Translating literally to "The Abode of Clouds," Meghalaya is a stunningly mountainous state in Northeast India. It separates the Assam valley from the plains of Bangladesh. The state is globally famous for holding the title of the wettest place on Earth, receiving phenomenal amounts of monsoon rainfall.

History & Culture

The state is primarily inhabited by the Khasi, Jaintia, and Garo tribes. Uniquely, these indigenous communities follow a matrilineal system, where lineage and inheritance are traced through the women of the family. The locals are world-renowned for bio-engineering the spectacular "Living Root Bridges" by guiding tree roots across rivers.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Meghalaya has a population of approximately 3.0 million. The economy is heavily dependent on agriculture (producing spices, fruits, and areca nuts) and mineral extraction, particularly coal and limestone.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Shillong: Often called the "Scotland of the East," known for its rolling hills, pine forests, and vibrant music scene.
  • Cherrapunji (Sohra) & Mawsynram: Famous for dramatic waterfalls (like Nohkalikai) and record-breaking rainfall.
  • Dawki: A border town famous for the Umngot River, which has water so crystal clear that boats appear to be floating in mid-air.
  • Mawlynnong: Famously awarded the title of the "Cleanest Village in Asia."

Languages Spoken

  • English (official state language)
  • Khasi and Garo (principal regional languages)
  • Pnar
