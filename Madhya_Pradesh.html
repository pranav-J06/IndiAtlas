Madhya Pradesh

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 November 1956

Capital: Bhopal

Geography & Climate

Often referred to as the "Heart of India" due to its central location, Madhya Pradesh is the second-largest state in the country by area. It is characterized by plateaus, the mighty Vindhya and Satpura mountain ranges, and dense forests. The sacred Narmada River is the primary geographical lifeline of the state.

History & Culture

Madhya Pradesh holds an unparalleled historical legacy, having been ruled by great ancient empires like the Mauryas, Guptas, and later the Marathas and Mughals. It is highly celebrated for its spectacular architecture, from the prehistoric rock shelters of Bhimbetka to the incredibly intricate Hindu and Jain temples at Khajuraho.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Madhya Pradesh has a population of approximately 72.6 million. Its economy is deeply agrarian, being one of the largest producers of soybeans and wheat in India. It also has significant mineral wealth, including diamond reserves in Panna.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Khajuraho Temples: UNESCO World Heritage site known globally for its stunning ancient temple art.
  • Kanha & Bandhavgarh National Parks: Famous tiger reserves that inspired Rudyard Kipling's "The Jungle Book."
  • Sanchi Stupa: An ancient Buddhist complex commissioned by Emperor Ashoka.
  • Gwalior & Ujjain: Cities steeped in immense historical, musical, and religious significance.

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official language and widely spoken)
  • Bundeli (spoken in the Bundelkhand region)
  • Malvi (spoken in the Malwa region)
  • Various tribal languages like Bhili and Gondi
