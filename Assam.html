Assam

Basic Information

Formed on: 26 January 1950

Capital: Dispur

Geography & Climate

Assam serves as the central gateway to Northeast India. The state's geography is defined by the massive, life-giving Brahmaputra River valley and the Barak River valley, bounded by beautiful rolling hills. It experiences a tropical monsoon climate with heavy rainfall.

History & Culture

Assam was ruled by the powerful Ahom Dynasty for 600 years, successfully resisting multiple Mughal invasions. The state's vibrant culture is best experienced through the energetic Bihu festivals, exquisite Muga silk weaving, and the ancient, highly revered Kamakhya Temple in Guwahati.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Assam has a population of approximately 31.2 million. Its economy is globally famous for producing some of the finest tea in the world (Assam Tea). It also possesses significant reserves of crude oil and natural gas.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Kaziranga National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage site and home to the world's largest population of the one-horned rhinoceros.
  • Majuli Island: The largest river island in the world, renowned for its Vaishnavite monasteries (Satras).
  • Guwahati: The largest city in the northeast, perched on the banks of the Brahmaputra.
  • Manas National Park: A spectacular biosphere reserve offering elephant safaris and diverse wildlife.

Languages Spoken

  • Assamese (official language)
  • Bodo (official language in the Bodoland Territorial Region)
  • Bengali (widely spoken in the Barak Valley)
  • Hindi and English
