Jammu and Kashmir

Basic Information

Reorganized as UT on: 31 October 2019

Capital: Srinagar (Summer), Jammu (Winter)

Geography & Climate

Located at the very top of India, this Union Territory encompasses the majestic Kashmir Valley and the Jammu region. It is surrounded by the Pir Panjal and Great Himalayan mountain ranges. The climate is alpine, featuring heavy winter snowfall in Kashmir and subtropical conditions in the Jammu plains.

History & Culture

Often described as "Paradise on Earth," the region has a complex and rich history blending Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic traditions. It is renowned for its exquisite handicrafts (Pashmina shawls, carpets), traditional Wazwan cuisine, and breathtaking natural beauty.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011 (prior to reorganization), the region had a population of approximately 12.5 million. The economy is deeply tied to agriculture (saffron, apples, walnuts), tourism, and traditional handlooms.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Srinagar: Famous for its iconic Dal Lake, Shikara boat rides, and Mughal Gardens.
  • Gulmarg: A premier destination for skiing and the world's highest operational cable car.
  • Mata Vaishno Devi: One of the most visited and revered Hindu pilgrimage shrines, located in Jammu.
  • Pahalgam & Sonamarg: Scenic valleys known for trekking and untouched natural beauty.

Languages Spoken

  • Kashmiri
  • Dogri (widely spoken in Jammu)
  • Urdu (official language)
  • Hindi and English
