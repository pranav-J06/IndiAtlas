Mizoram

Basic Information

Formed on: 20 February 1987

Capital: Aizawl

Geography & Climate

Translating to "Land of the Hill People," Mizoram sits in the southernmost tip of Northeast India, sharing international borders with Myanmar and Bangladesh. The state is a stunning canvas of steep, rolling green hills, plunging gorges, and dense bamboo forests. The Tropic of Cancer passes directly through it.

History & Culture

Mizo culture is deeply rooted in a closely-knit community structure governed by a code of ethics known as "Tlawmngaihna," which mandates kindness, hospitality, and unselfishness. The state is famous for its vibrant festivals like Chapchar Kut and the incredibly fast-paced, rhythmic Cheraw (bamboo) dance.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Mizoram has a population of approximately 1.1 million and boasts one of the highest literacy rates in India. The economy revolves around agriculture, horticulture, and the massive production of bamboo and handloom products.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Aizawl: A picturesque capital city built on steep ridges, offering spectacular views of the surrounding valleys.
  • Vantawng Falls: The highest and most breathtaking waterfall in the state.
  • Phawngpui (Blue Mountain): The highest peak in Mizoram, renowned for rare flora, fauna, and panoramic views.
  • Reiek: A heritage village that perfectly preserves traditional Mizo culture and architecture.

Languages Spoken

  • Mizo (official and most widely spoken language)
  • English (used in education and official administration)
  • Hindi
