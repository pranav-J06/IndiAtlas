Uttar Pradesh

Basic Information

Formed on: 26 January 1950

Capital: Lucknow

Geography & Climate

Located in northern India, Uttar Pradesh covers a massive area of the highly fertile Indo-Gangetic Plain. The state is defined by the mighty rivers that flow through it, most notably the Ganges and the Yamuna.

History & Culture

Uttar Pradesh is often considered the cultural heartland of India. It is the birthplace of Lord Rama (Ayodhya) and Lord Krishna (Mathura). It was the seat of power for the majestic Mughal Empire and played a massive role in India's independence movement. It is home to classical music, Kathak dance, and incredible Nawabi cuisine.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Uttar Pradesh is the most populous state in India with approximately 199.8 million people. Its economy is heavily based on agriculture, being the largest producer of food grains and sugarcane in India, alongside growing IT hubs like Noida.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Taj Mahal, Agra: One of the Seven Wonders of the World, built by Emperor Shah Jahan.
  • Varanasi (Kashi): One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and a major spiritual center.
  • Ayodhya & Mathura: Highly sacred ancient cities drawing millions of pilgrims.
  • Lucknow: The "City of Nawabs," famous for its refined culture, monuments like Bara Imambara, and food.

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official language)
  • Urdu (additional official language)
  • Awadhi, Bhojpuri, and Braj Bhasha (major regional dialects)
