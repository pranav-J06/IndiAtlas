Tamil Nadu

Basic Information

Formed on: 26 January 1950

Capital: Chennai

Geography & Climate

Located at the southernmost tip of the Indian peninsula, Tamil Nadu is bordered by the Eastern Ghats, the Nilgiri Hills, the Bay of Bengal, and the Indian Ocean. It generally experiences a tropical climate and receives rain primarily from the northeast monsoon.

History & Culture

Tamil Nadu is the bastion of Dravidian culture and one of the oldest surviving classical civilizations in the world. It was ruled by powerful ancient dynasties like the Cholas, Cheras, and Pandyas. The state is famous for its towering, colorful temple architecture (Gopurams), classical Carnatic music, and the ancient Bharatanatyam dance form.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Tamil Nadu has a population of approximately 72.1 million. It is one of the most industrialized and urbanized states in India, with a powerhouse economy heavily driven by automobile manufacturing, textiles, software services, and engineering.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Chennai: The bustling capital, home to Marina Beach and historic colonial architecture.
  • Madurai: An ancient city centered around the breathtaking Meenakshi Amman Temple.
  • Ooty & Kodaikanal: Popular, misty hill stations nestled in the Western Ghats.
  • Kanyakumari: The southernmost tip of mainland India, where three major water bodies meet.

Languages Spoken

  • Tamil (official language, one of the oldest classical languages in the world)
  • English (widely used in business, education, and administration)
  • Telugu and Malayalam (in border areas)
