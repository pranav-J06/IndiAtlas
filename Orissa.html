Odisha (formerly Orissa)

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 April 1936 (Utkal Divas)

Capital: Bhubaneswar

Geography & Climate

Situated on the eastern coast along the Bay of Bengal, Odisha features a long coastline, the fertile delta of the Mahanadi river, and the rolling hills of the Eastern Ghats. It is also home to Chilika Lake, the largest brackish water lagoon in Asia.

History & Culture

Odisha is the heart of the ancient Kalinga Empire. Its history was famously transformed when Emperor Ashoka, after the bloody Kalinga War, renounced violence and embraced Buddhism. The state is celebrated for its spectacular ancient temple architecture, traditional appliqué art (Pipli), and the graceful Odissi classical dance.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Odisha has a population of approximately 41.9 million. It is incredibly rich in natural resources, holding a massive share of India's iron ore, bauxite, and coal reserves, making it a major hub for steel and aluminum production.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Puri: Home to the sacred Jagannath Temple and the massive annual Rath Yatra festival.
  • Konark Sun Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage site shaped like a giant stone chariot.
  • Bhubaneswar: Known as the "Temple City of India," featuring ancient structures like the Lingaraj Temple.
  • Chilika Lake: A biodiversity hotspot famous for migratory birds and Irrawaddy dolphins.

Languages Spoken

  • Odia (official language)
  • Hindi
  • Telugu (in southern border regions)
  • Santali (spoken by tribal populations)
