Himachal Pradesh

Basic Information

Formed on: 25 January 1971

Capital: Shimla (Summer), Dharamshala (Winter)

Geography & Climate

Nestled in the western Himalayas, Himachal Pradesh translates to "In the Lap of Himalayas." The state is characterized by extreme mountainous terrain, deep valleys, and perennial rivers. The climate varies from pleasant in the valleys to extreme freezing temperatures at higher altitudes.

History & Culture

Known as the "Land of Gods," the state is dotted with ancient stone and wooden temples. Dharamshala is globally recognized as the residence of the Dalai Lama and the center of the exiled Tibetan government, bringing a rich Buddhist influence to the region.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Himachal Pradesh has a population of approximately 6.9 million. The economy relies heavily on hydroelectric power generation, tourism, and agriculture—particularly its famous apple orchards.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Shimla & Manali: Extremely popular hill stations famous for snow, colonial architecture, and adventure sports.
  • Dharamshala & McLeod Ganj: Centers of Tibetan culture and spirituality.
  • Dalhousie: A quiet, picturesque town retaining its colonial-era charm.
  • Spiti Valley: A stunning, high-altitude cold desert known for ancient Buddhist monasteries.

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official language)
  • Pahari dialects (regional languages spoken by the majority)
  • English (education and tourism)
