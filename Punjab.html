Punjab

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 November 1966 (current boundaries)

Capital: Chandigarh

Geography & Climate

Located in northwestern India, the name Punjab translates to "Land of Five Rivers" (referring to the Sutlej, Beas, Ravi, Chenab, and Jhelum). The state is composed of incredibly fertile alluvial plains and experiences an extreme climate with blazing summers and cold winters.

History & Culture

Punjab is the birthplace of Sikhism, founded in the 15th century by Guru Nanak. The culture is globally recognized for its vibrant energy, hearty cuisine, and the energetic Bhangra and Giddha folk dances. It also bears the profound historical scars of the 1947 Partition of India.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Punjab has a population of approximately 27.7 million. Often called the "Granary of India," its economy is heavily dominated by agriculture, being one of the largest producers of wheat and rice in the country thanks to the Green Revolution.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Amritsar: The spiritual heart of the state, home to the magnificent Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib).
  • Jallianwala Bagh: A poignant memorial to the tragic 1919 massacre.
  • Wagah Border: Famous for the daily, high-energy military retreat ceremony on the India-Pakistan border.
  • Anandpur Sahib: One of the most important sacred cities in Sikhism, known for the vibrant Hola Mohalla festival.

Languages Spoken

  • Punjabi (official language)
  • Hindi (common for inter-state communication)
  • English (used in education and administration)
