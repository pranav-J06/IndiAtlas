Arunachal Pradesh

Basic Information

Formed on: 20 February 1987

Capital: Itanagar

Geography & Climate

Known as the "Land of Dawn-Lit Mountains," it is the easternmost state of India, greeting the first rays of the morning sun. It shares international borders with Bhutan, China, and Myanmar. The state is entirely mountainous, covered in pristine forests, deep river valleys, and snow-capped peaks.

History & Culture

The state is incredibly ethnically diverse, home to over 26 major tribes (including the Monpa, Nyishi, and Apatani), each with rich, distinct traditions, festivals, and weaving styles. It is also a deeply spiritual center, housing the Tawang Monastery, the largest Buddhist monastery in India.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, it has a sparse population of approximately 1.38 million. The economy is primarily based on agriculture (jhum cultivation), forestry, and holds massive, largely untapped hydroelectric power potential.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Tawang: A breathtaking high-altitude town famous for its massive monastery and glacial lakes.
  • Ziro Valley: A lush green valley recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for the Ziro Music Festival.
  • Namdapha National Park: A massive biodiversity hotspot where snow leopards, clouded leopards, and tigers coexist.
  • Itanagar: The capital city, featuring the historical Ita Fort and vibrant local markets.

Languages Spoken

  • English (official language)
  • Hindi (widely used as a lingua franca)
  • Dozens of indigenous tribal languages (Nyishi, Adi, Apatani, Monpa)
