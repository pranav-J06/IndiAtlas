Gujarat

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 May 1960

Capital: Gandhinagar

Geography & Climate

Located on the western coast of India, Gujarat boasts the longest coastline of any Indian state. Its geography is incredibly diverse, featuring the vast salt deserts of the Rann of Kutch, the Saurashtra peninsula, and fertile plains in the south.

History & Culture

Gujarat is historically significant as a major hub of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization (with sites like Lothal and Dholavira). It is famously the birthplace of Mahatma Gandhi. The state is globally celebrated for its vibrant festivals like Navratri and its traditional Garba dance.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Gujarat has a population of approximately 60 million. It is one of India's most highly industrialized and economically prosperous states, leading the country in manufacturing, diamond cutting, and petrochemicals.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Statue of Unity: The tallest statue in the world, dedicated to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.
  • Gir National Park: The last and only natural habitat of the majestic Asiatic Lion.
  • Somnath Temple: An ancient and highly revered Hindu temple located on the coast.
  • Rann of Kutch: A massive seasonal salt marsh known for the vibrant Rann Utsav festival.

Languages Spoken

  • Gujarati (official and widely spoken language)
  • Hindi (common for urban and inter-state communication)
  • Kutchi (spoken in the Kutch region)
  • English (used in education and business)
