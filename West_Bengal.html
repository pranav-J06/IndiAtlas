West Bengal

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 November 1956

Capital: Kolkata (formerly Calcutta)

Geography & Climate

West Bengal acts as a geographical bottleneck connecting Northeast India to the rest of the country. It is the only state in India that stretches from the towering Himalayas in the north (Darjeeling) all the way down to the Bay of Bengal in the south.

History & Culture

The state was the epicenter of the Indian Renaissance and the early Indian independence movement. It holds an immense cultural legacy, boasting Nobel laureates in literature and science. The culture is famous for its profound appreciation of arts, poetry, cinema, and the grand, artistic celebration of the Durga Puja festival.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, West Bengal has a population of approximately 91.3 million. It has a diverse economy driven by agriculture (being the largest producer of rice and jute in India), tea production, mining, and a wide array of medium-scale industries.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Kolkata: The cultural capital of India, known for the Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge, and street food.
  • Darjeeling: A world-famous hill station known for its expansive tea gardens and the heritage toy train.
  • Sundarbans: The largest mangrove forest in the world, home to the elusive Royal Bengal Tiger.
  • Kalimpong: A quiet, beautiful hill town known for orchids and ancient Buddhist monasteries.

Languages Spoken

  • Bengali (official language)
  • Hindi (common in urban areas)
  • English (widely used in business and education)
  • Nepali (official in the northern Darjeeling hills)
