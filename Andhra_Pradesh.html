Andhra Pradesh & Telangana

Basic Information

Andhra Pradesh formed on: 1 November 1956

Telangana formed on: 2 June 2014 (carved out of AP)

Capitals: Amaravati (Andhra Pradesh), Hyderabad (Telangana)

Geography & Climate

Together, these states cover a vast area of southeastern India. Andhra Pradesh boasts the second-longest coastline in India along the Bay of Bengal, while Telangana sits on the rugged Deccan Plateau. Both states are nourished by the mighty Godavari and Krishna rivers.

History & Culture

The region has a glorious past, ruled by the ancient Satavahanas, the Kakatiyas, and the Nizams of Hyderabad. The culture is celebrated through the exquisite Kuchipudi classical dance, incredibly spicy and flavorful Telugu cuisine, and rich literary traditions.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, the combined population is approximately 84 million. The region is often called the "Rice Bowl of India" due to its massive agricultural output. Hyderabad is a global powerhouse for information technology, pharmaceuticals, and biotechnology.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Tirupati (AP): Home to the Sri Venkateswara Temple, one of the wealthiest and most visited religious sites on Earth.
  • Hyderabad (TG): The "City of Pearls," famous for the iconic Charminar and Golconda Fort.
  • Araku Valley (AP): A stunning, misty hill station known for its coffee plantations.
  • Warangal (TG): An ancient capital city filled with spectacular Kakatiya-era architecture.

Languages Spoken

  • Telugu (official and mother tongue for the vast majority)
  • Urdu (widely spoken in Hyderabad and Telangana)
  • Hindi and English
