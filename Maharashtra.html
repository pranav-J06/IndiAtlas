Maharashtra

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 May 1960 (Maharashtra Day)

Capital: Mumbai (Winter Capital: Nagpur)

Geography & Climate

Maharashtra spans the western and central parts of the country, featuring a long coastline along the Arabian Sea. It is characterized by the rugged Western Ghats (Sahyadri range) and the vast, fertile Deccan Plateau. The major rivers that flow through the state include the Godavari and the Krishna.

Rich History & Culture

The region has a profound historical legacy, most notably associated with the Maratha Empire founded by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in the 17th century. The state is renowned for its vibrant culture, including traditional folk dances like Lavani, the grand celebration of Ganesh Chaturthi, and its massive contribution to Indian cinema (Bollywood).

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Maharashtra has a population of approximately 112 million, making it the second-most populous state in India. It is a major economic powerhouse, contributing heavily to the national GDP through finance, entertainment, IT, and agriculture.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Mumbai: The bustling financial capital, home to the Gateway of India.
  • Ajanta and Ellora Caves: UNESCO World Heritage sites famous for ancient rock-cut architecture.
  • Lonavala & Mahabaleshwar: Scenic hill stations nestled in the Western Ghats.
  • Pune: Known as the "Oxford of the East" and a historical center of the Peshwas.

Languages Spoken

  • Marathi (official language and mother tongue for the majority)
  • Hindi (widely spoken, especially in urban areas)
  • English (used in education, business, and administration)
  • Konkani (spoken in the coastal Konkan region)
