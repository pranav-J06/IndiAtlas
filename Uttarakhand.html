Uttarakhand

Basic Information

Formed on: 9 November 2000

Capital: Dehradun (Winter), Gairsain (Summer)

Geography & Climate

Carved out of northern Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand is heavily mountainous, encompassing a large section of the majestic Himalayas. It is ecologically rich, heavily forested, and holds the sacred sources of India's most vital rivers: the Ganges (Gangotri) and the Yamuna (Yamunotri).

History & Culture

Referred to as "Devbhoomi" (Land of the Gods), the state's culture is deeply intertwined with Hinduism. It hosts some of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India, including the Char Dham. It is also globally famous for its yoga centers and deep spiritual roots.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Uttarakhand has a population of approximately 10.1 million. The economy is heavily sustained by religious and adventure tourism, hydroelectric power generation, and agriculture in the lower valleys.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Rishikesh: Known as the "Yoga Capital of the World" and famous for white-water rafting.
  • Nainital & Mussoorie: Classic, scenic hill stations popular for their lakes and colonial charm.
  • Haridwar: A deeply holy city where the Ganges exits the mountains, famous for the evening Ganga Aarti.
  • Jim Corbett National Park: India's oldest national park, renowned for its Bengal tigers.

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official language)
  • Sanskrit (second official language)
  • Garhwali and Kumaoni (the primary regional languages spoken in the mountains)
