Rajasthan

Basic Information

Formed on: 30 March 1949

Capital: Jaipur

Geography & Climate

Rajasthan is the largest state in India by land area. It is characterized by the vast, arid expanse of the Thar Desert in the west and the ancient Aravalli Mountain Range running through its center. The climate is generally hot and dry.

History & Culture

Translating to "Land of Kings," Rajasthan has an incredibly rich historical tapestry woven by the valorous Rajput dynasties. It is world-renowned for its majestic hilltop forts, opulent palaces, vibrant traditional clothing, and deeply rooted folk music and puppetry traditions.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Rajasthan has a population of approximately 68 million. Its economy relies heavily on agriculture, tourism, and mining—it is a leading producer of marble, sandstone, and various precious gems in India.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Jaipur: The "Pink City," famous for the Hawa Mahal, Amer Fort, and bustling bazaars.
  • Udaipur: The "City of Lakes," known for its romantic, floating Lake Palace.
  • Jaisalmer: The "Golden City," featuring a massive living fort rising out of the desert sands.
  • Pushkar: A holy city known for its sacred lake and the massive annual camel fair.

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official language)
  • Rajasthani and its dialects (Marwari, Mewari)
  • English (widely used in the tourism sector)
