Kerala

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 November 1956

Capital: Thiruvananthapuram

Geography & Climate

Tucked between the Arabian Sea and the Western Ghats, Kerala is famous for its intricate network of coastal backwaters, tranquil beaches, and lush, green highlands. It experiences a wet, tropical climate heavily influenced by the seasonal monsoons.

History & Culture

Often referred to as "God's Own Country," Kerala was a prominent spice exporter since 3000 BCE, attracting traders from Rome, Arabia, and Europe. It is famous for its holistic Ayurvedic healing traditions, classical performing arts like Kathakali, and thrilling traditional boat races.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Kerala has a population of approximately 33 million. It holds the distinction of having the highest literacy rate and life expectancy in India. The economy relies heavily on remittances from overseas workers, tourism, and the export of spices, tea, and rubber.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Alappuzha (Alleppey): The hub of the famous Kerala backwaters, known for houseboat cruises.
  • Munnar: A breathtakingly green hill station covered in rolling tea estates.
  • Kochi (Cochin): A historic port city featuring Jewish synagogues and iconic Chinese fishing nets.
  • Wayanad: Known for its spice plantations, wildlife reserves, and ancient caves.

Languages Spoken

  • Malayalam (official language)
  • English (widely understood due to high literacy and tourism)
  • Tamil (spoken in border regions)
