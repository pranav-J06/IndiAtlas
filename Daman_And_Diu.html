Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu

Basic Information

Merged as a single UT on: 26 January 2020

Capital: Daman

Geography & Climate

This Union Territory comprises distinct geographical areas on and near the western coast of India, near Gujarat. Daman is a small enclave on the coast, Diu is a beautiful island off the Kathiawar peninsula, and Dadra and Nagar Haveli are landlocked regions sandwiched between Maharashtra and Gujarat.

History & Culture

These areas were former Portuguese colonies and were integrated into the Indian Union in the mid-20th century (1961 for Daman and Diu, 1954 for Dadra and Nagar Haveli). The Portuguese influence is still heavily visible today in the region's spectacular architecture, churches, and laid-back coastal culture.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, the combined population is approximately 0.59 million. The economy is heavily driven by manufacturing industries (due to tax incentives), fishing, and a thriving tourism sector.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Diu Fort: A massive, well-preserved 16th-century Portuguese fortress overlooking the sea.
  • Silvassa: Known for its lush gardens, tribal museum, and wildlife sanctuaries.
  • Devka & Nagoa Beaches: Popular coastal spots offering relaxation and water sports.
  • St. Paul’s Church: A stunning example of baroque architecture in Diu.

Languages Spoken

  • Gujarati (most widely spoken)
  • Hindi
  • Marathi
  • English and Portuguese (historical presence)
