Bihar

Basic Information

Formed on: 22 March 1912

Capital: Patna

Geography & Climate

Located in eastern India, Bihar is completely landlocked and is bisected west-to-east by the holy Ganges River. This creates the incredibly fertile Indo-Gangetic plains, making it one of the most agriculturally rich regions in the country.

History & Culture

Bihar is the cradle of ancient Indian empires, most notably the mighty Magadha and Maurya empires. It is the birthplace of Buddhism and Jainism. The state is globally famous for the ancient Nalanda University (one of the world's first residential universities), exquisite Madhubani art, and the massive Chhath Puja festival.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Bihar has a population of approximately 104.1 million, making it the third-most populous state in India. The economy is predominantly agricultural, producing massive yields of vegetables, fruits (like litchis), and grains.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Bodh Gaya: The most sacred site in Buddhism, where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree.
  • Nalanda: The spectacular, sprawling ruins of the ancient center of higher learning.
  • Rajgir: An ancient city surrounded by hills, known for its hot springs and the Vishwa Shanti Stupa.
  • Vaishali: Considered one of the first republics in the world and deeply connected to Lord Mahavira.

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official language)
  • Bhojpuri, Maithili, and Magahi (major regional languages)
  • Urdu (additional official language)
