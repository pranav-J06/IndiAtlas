Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Basic Information

Established as UT: 1 November 1956

Capital: Port Blair

Geography & Climate

Situated at the juncture of the Bay of Bengal and the Andaman Sea, this majestic archipelago comprises over 500 islands, though only about 37 are inhabited. The islands feature lush tropical rainforests, pristine white-sand beaches, and vibrant coral reefs.

History & Culture

The islands are home to some of the world's most isolated indigenous tribes, including the Sentinelese and Jarawas, who have lived there for tens of thousands of years. Historically, Port Blair is known for the infamous Cellular Jail (Kala Pani), where Indian freedom fighters were exiled during British rule.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, the population is approximately 0.38 million. The local economy is largely driven by agriculture, fishing, and a rapidly expanding eco-tourism and water-sports industry.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Port Blair: The capital city, home to historical museums and the Cellular Jail National Memorial.
  • Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep): Globally famous for Radhanagar Beach and scuba diving.
  • Neil Island (Shaheed Dweep): A tranquil island known for its beautiful biodiversity and relaxed vibe.
  • Baratang Island: Famous for its unique limestone caves and mud volcanoes.

Languages Spoken

  • Hindi (official and most widely spoken)
  • Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, and Malayalam
  • English
