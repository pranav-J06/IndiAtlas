Karnataka

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 November 1956

Capital: Bengaluru

Geography & Climate

Located in southwestern India, Karnataka boasts a highly diverse geography. It features the coastal Karavali region along the Arabian Sea, the lush, mountainous Malenadu region (Western Ghats), and the vast Deccan plateau (Bayalu Seeme).

History & Culture

Karnataka has been home to some of ancient and medieval India's most powerful empires, including the Chalukyas, Hoysalas, and the magnificent Vijayanagara Empire. It has a rich tradition in Carnatic music, literature, and produces the majority of the nation's coffee and silk.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Karnataka has a population of approximately 61.1 million. Its capital, Bengaluru, is known globally as the "Silicon Valley of India," driving a massive IT and aerospace economy alongside traditional agriculture.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Bengaluru: A modern tech hub known for its beautiful parks and gardens.
  • Hampi: The spectacular, boulder-strewn ruins of the ancient Vijayanagara Empire.
  • Mysuru (Mysore): Famous for the incredibly ornate Mysore Palace and the Dasara festival.
  • Coorg (Kodagu): A misty hill station famous for its sprawling coffee plantations.

Languages Spoken

  • Kannada (official language and mother tongue for the majority)
  • Tulu and Konkani (spoken heavily in the coastal regions)
  • Urdu (common in urban areas)
  • English (widely used in business and tech)
