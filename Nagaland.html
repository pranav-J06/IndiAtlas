Nagaland

Basic Information

Formed on: 1 December 1963

Capital: Kohima

Geography & Climate

Located in the extreme northeast of India, Nagaland shares an international border with Myanmar. It is an almost entirely mountainous state covered in rich, dense flora and fauna. The state enjoys a pleasant, sub-tropical climate.

History & Culture

Nagaland is known as the "Land of Festivals" and is home to 16 major indigenous tribes, each with its own distinct language, traditional dress, and customs. The state is globally famous for the annual Hornbill Festival, a vibrant celebration of tribal heritage. Kohima also holds immense historical significance as the site of a pivotal World War II battle.

Economy & Population

As per Census 2011, Nagaland has a population of approximately 2.0 million. The economy is primarily agrarian, relying on terrace farming and the traditional "jhum" (shifting) cultivation, alongside forestry and handloom weaving.

Popular Tourist Places

  • Kohima: The capital city, home to the poignant WWII War Cemetery.
  • Dimapur: The commercial hub and site of ancient Kachari ruins.
  • Dzukou Valley: A breathtaking, high-altitude valley famous for its lush greenery and seasonal flowers.
  • Mon: The cultural heartland of the Konyak tribe, known for their traditional wood carving and history.

Languages Spoken

  • English (official state language)
  • Nagamese (a Creole language acting as the primary lingua franca)
  • Various indigenous tribal languages like Ao, Angami, and Sema
